Reading List on Government Responsiveness

    li>Putnam, Robert D. 1993. Making Democracy Work: Civic Traditions in Modern Italy. Princeton, NJ: Princeton Univ. Press.
  • O’Brien, Kevin J., and Lianjiang Li. 2006. Rightful Resistance in Rural China. New York: Cambridge University Press.
  • Chen, Xi. 2009. “The Power of ‘Troublemaking’: Protest Tactics and Their Efficacy in China.” Comparative Politics 41 (4): 451–71. https://doi.org/10.5129/001041509X12911362972557.
  • Chen, Xi. 2012. Social Protest and Contentious Authoritarianism in China. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
  • King, Gary, Jennifer Pan, and Margaret E. Roberts. 2013. “How Censorship in China Allows Government Criticism but Silences Collective Expression.” American Political Science Review 107 (2): 326–43. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055413000014.

  • Chen, Jidong, and Yiqing Xu. 2017. “Why Do Authoritarian Regimes Allow Citizens to Voice Opinions Publicly?” The Journal of Politics 79 (3): 792–803. https://doi.org/10.1086/690303.
  • Distelhorst, Greg, and Yue Hou. 2017. “Constituency Service Under Nondemocratic Rule: Evidence from China.” The Journal of Politics 79 (3): 1024–40. https://doi.org/10.1086/690948.

  • Cai, Yongshun, and Titi Zhou. 2019. “Online Political Participation in China: Local Government and Differentiated Response.” The China Quarterly 238 (June): 331–52. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0305741019000055.
  • Jiang, Junyan, Tianguang Meng, and Qing Zhang. 2019. “From Internet to social safety net: The policy consequences of online participation in China.” Governance 32(3): 531–546.
  • Jiang, Junyan, and Yu Zeng. 2020. “Countering Capture: Elite Networks and Government Responsiveness in China’s Land Market Reform.” The Journal of Politics 82 (1): 13–28. https://doi.org/10.1086/705595.
  • Dimitar D. Gueorguiev. 2022. Retrofitting Leninism: Participation Without Democracy in China. New York: Oxford University Press.
  • Zeng, Yu, Junyan Jiang, Jie Li, and Christian Göbel. 2023. “The Rise of Grassroots Civil Society Under One-Party Rule: The Case of China’s Homeowner Associations.” World Politics 75 (3): 608–46. https://doi.org/10.1353/wp.2023.a900714.
  • Wang, Howard H., Edmund W. Cheng, Xi Chen, and Hai Liang. 2024. “How Institutionalized Feedback Works: Online Citizen Complaints and Local Government Responsiveness in China.” Governance, 1–24. https://doi.org/10.1111/gove.12907.
