Teaching Experience and Feedback

I hold a strong commitment to teaching excellence. At CUHK, I served as a teaching assistant for three courses. I was praised for simplifying complex quantitative methods and fostering student engagement. For instance, a student commented:

“thank you, Howard, for teaching the quantitative methods and providing clear guidelines. He has been a very supportive tutor and one of the best tutors I have ever encountered.”

Teaching Philosophy

  • Students must work hard, which in turn pushes teachers to share more knowledge. (Prof. Lianjiang LI's teaching motto)
  • Always stay open. (Prof. Xi CHEN's teaching motto)
  • Always keep learning new and challenging things. (Prof. Hai LIANG's teaching motto)
  • Methods are weapons.
  • The essence of teaching is to pass on responsibility. This responsibility comes from the inspiration received from my teachers when I was a student and the duties entrusted to teachers by society.
  • Only by understanding something yourself can you truly teach it to your students.

Computational Social Science Course

I am interested in teaching a computational social science course. I have preliminarily designed a course titled “Computational Social Science: Introduction and Application.” It covers:

  • R/Python basics
  • Web scraping
  • Machine learning (e.g., topic modeling and supervised learning)
  • Social network analysis
  • Image-as-data
  • Audio/video analysis
  • How Computational Social Science contributes to traditional methods (e.g., survey approach)
  • And other related topics

Course Evaluation (100 Points in Total)

  1. R or Python Coding – 10 Points
  2. Web Scraping – 10 Points
  3. Text-as-Data – 10 Points
  4. Image, Audio, or Video as Data – 10 Points
  5. Social Network Analysis – 10 Points
  6. Statistical Regression – 10 Points
  7. Final Course Paper – 40 Points

Requirements for Final Course Paper

  1. Clear Research Question: Define a focused and compelling research question that addresses a significant issue in computational social science.
  2. Literature Review: Provide a thorough review of existing studies, demonstrating an understanding of the current state of knowledge in the field.
  3. Research Gap and Theoretical Argument: Identify a clear gap in the literature and present a strong theoretical argument.
  4. Data: Describe the data source(s) used, including how the data was collected, and ensure the dataset is appropriate for answering the research question.
  5. Variable Measurement: Clearly define and justify the measurement of key variables, including dependent, independent, and control variables.
  6. Model Specification: Specify the analytical model(s) used, ensuring they are appropriate to answer the research question and suited to the nature of the data.
  7. Main Results: Present and interpret the main findings in a clear and organized manner, supported by appropriate visualizations (e.g., tables, graphs).
  8. Robustness Checks: Conduct and report robustness checks to ensure the validity of results (e.g., measurement bias, sample selection bias, mechanisms, etc.).
  9. Theoretical Contribution: Clearly articulate the broader theoretical implications of the findings, emphasizing their relevance to a wider audience.
